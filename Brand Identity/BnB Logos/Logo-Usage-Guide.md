# Logo Usage Guide — Bored N Buzzed

> **Direction:** Keep + Extend. The existing logo system is retained in full — it's authentic, lifestyle-rooted, and culturally credible. The extension is the design system deployed around it: warm dark backgrounds, elevated gold, deep navy, and intentional color pairings that make the existing marks feel premium.

## The Two Logo Systems

### System A — The Wordmark
The graffiti-influenced custom lettering. Bold, expressive, street-rooted. This is the primary brand logomark.

**Character:** Confident, lifestyle, cultural. The "Bored Ape" DNA is in this lettering — chunky, saturated, unapologetic.

**When to use:** Website header, social profiles, in-store signage, packaging, merch.

### System B — The Badge / Seal
The circular coaster format with the Bored Ape character, "TOTEM LAKE" location call-out, and QR code ring. Cream/off-white palette.

**Character:** Community, lifestyle, place-specific. The strongest ambassador asset B&B has — it telegraphs who they are in one image.

**When to use:** Events, coasters, stickers, vendor day collateral, in-store merchandise, social lifestyle content, community pop-up branding.

## Clearspace & Sizing

### Wordmark
- Minimum clearspace: equal to the height of the "B" in "BORED" on all sides
- Minimum digital size: 120px wide
- Minimum print size: 1.5 inches wide
- Never stretch, skew, or rotate the wordmark

### Badge
- Minimum digital size: 80px diameter
- Minimum print size: 1 inch diameter
- Clearspace: equal to 10% of badge diameter on all sides
- The badge is always circular — never crop or mask into a non-circular shape

---

## Never Do

- Never apply a drop shadow to either logo on dark backgrounds
- Never place the black wordmark on dark backgrounds without inverting it
- Never use the logo at low contrast (light wordmark on light bg, dark on dark)
- Never stretch, skew, outline, or add effects to the wordmark letterforms
- Never crop the badge — it functions as a complete circular unit
- Never place the wordmark over a busy photographic background without a solid color bar or overlay
- Never use the wordmark and badge at the same visual weight in the same asset — one leads, one supports

## Extension: New Premium Deployment Contexts

These are the "extend" additions — new contexts the brand hasn't used the logos in before.

### Website Header (dark treatment)
- Background: `#0D0D0B`
- Logo: White horizontal wordmark (Facebook Cover.png)
- Supporting: DM Sans navigation links in `#8D867A`, gold `#C9A84C` on active state
- Height: 64px header, logo at 32px height

### Digital Display Loop (in-store screens)
- Background: Full-bleed `#0D0D0B` or `#0F2640`
- Logo: White wordmark centered or top-left
- Badge: Bottom-right as location identifier
- Gold accent bar: thin `#C9A84C` horizontal rule

### Social Profile (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook)
- Profile avatar: Badge (Coaster front) — already circular, perfect fit
- Bio header/highlight covers: White wordmark on `#0D0D0B`

### Event Collateral (Vendor Days, Pop-ups)
- Badge as hero mark — large, centered, cream version on warm dark bg
- Wordmark as secondary identifier — top or bottom, smaller scale
- Gold ring treatment on badge for special events

### Merch / Physical (coasters, bags, apparel)
- Black wordmark on natural/cream stock — keeps the lifestyle feel
- Badge as primary graphic on coasters, stickers, sleeve prints
- Gold foil treatment on black stock for premium merch tier

### Price Cards / In-Store Signage
- White wordmark on `#0D0D0B` header strip
- JetBrains Mono for all product data below
- Gold `#C9A84C` as accent on featured products
