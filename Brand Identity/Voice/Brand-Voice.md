# Brand Voice — Bored N Buzzed Eastside Premium

> Built from: NotebookLM market synthesis + full-site audit (34 pages) + design-brief sprint
> Core insight: *"The Culture's Outpost in the Suburbs."*

---

## Voice in One Line

**Confident without arrogance. Cultural without trying. Eastside-specific, always.**

We speak like someone who genuinely knows this market, this community, and this product — not like a brand performing knowledge. We don't need to convince you. We invite you.

## Five Voice Principles

### 1. Be specific. Always.
Generic copy is cheap. Specific copy is credible.

> **Not this:** "Premium cannabis products handpicked for quality."
> **This:** "Pineapple Chunk from Sky High Gardens — pesticide-free, hand-trimmed, LED-grown in Auburn. 27.4% THC. Ask our team how it smokes."

Specificity is a premium signal. Anyone can say "quality." Only we can say *this product, from this farm, with these numbers.*

### 2. Speak with cultural authority, not borrowed slang.
We've earned credibility in this market. We don't need to perform it with stoner vocabulary.

> **Not this:** "CATCH US OUTSIDE, HOW BOUT THAT? Get turnt on Wax Wednesday."
> **This:** "Concentrates worth talking about. Wednesday, we go deeper."

We know the culture. We don't need to announce it.

### 3. Root everything in the Eastside.
We're not a generic Seattle dispensary. We're *Kirkland's* dispensary. The Eastside market belongs to us — name it.

> **Not this:** "Serving the greater Seattle area with top-tier cannabis."
> **This:** "Kirkland's standard for cannabis. Fifteen minutes from Redmond. Worth the drive from Bothell."

Name places. Reference landmarks. Make the geography a feature.

### 4. Let the work speak. Skip the hype.
Premium brands don't tell you they're premium. They demonstrate it through specificity, restraint, and authority.

> **Not this:** "THE BEST SELECTION. THE BEST PRICES. THE BEST EXPERIENCE."
> **This:** "We carry what we'd buy ourselves. If it doesn't pass our own standard, it doesn't make our shelf."

Trust the product. Trust the curation. Trust the reader.

### 5. Deals support the brand. The brand doesn't serve the deals.
Discounts exist. They don't define us. When we mention a deal, it's an invitation — not a pitch.

> **Not this:** "TAKE 30% OFF ANY 3 FLOWER PRODUCTS. TODAY ONLY."
> **This:** "Flower Friday. Three strains, 30% off. A good reason to come in and ask what's new."

## Voice Modes

Different contexts require different registers. All of them stay on-brand.

### Editorial (Website hero, social headers, brand moments)
**Tone:** Authoritative, spare, confident. Short sentences. Active verbs. No qualifiers.

> "The Eastside standard for cannabis. Set in Kirkland."

> "Hand-picked. Locally known. Hard to match."

> "We've been here. We'll be here. Come find out why."

### Warm + Conversational (Product descriptions, rewards, in-store signage)
**Tone:** Knowledgeable friend who happens to run the best shop on the block. Specific, warm, never condescending.

> "Himalayan Blackberry from Soulshine — earthy, a little sweet, hits smooth. This one's consistent batch to batch, which matters more than people think. Ask us about the terpene profile if you want the full picture."

> "Joining rewards is simple. Every dollar you spend earns a point. No math homework, no confusing tiers."

### Educational (Blog, Cannabis 101, staff binder)
**Tone:** Clear, respectful, actually informative. We assume the reader is smart. We explain without condescending.

> "Terpenes are why two strains at the same THC percentage can feel completely different. It's not the cannabinoids doing all the work — it's the full chemical picture. Here's how to read it."

### Event / Community (Jim Jones recap style, Vendor Days, pop-ups)
**Tone:** This is where the cultural voice fully opens. Warmer, more personal. Reference real moments.

> "At the end of the day, it wasn't just an event — it was a reminder of why we do what we do. We're here for the people, the culture, and the moments that bring us together. Stay tuned, Kirkland. This is just the beginning."

*(This is the best copy currently on the site — the Jim Jones recap. Capture this register.)*

## The Words We Use

### Use
- Craft · Curated · Hand-picked · Specific strain/brand names · Eastside · Kirkland · Our team
- "We stand behind" · "Ask us" · "Worth knowing" · "Consistent" · "The honest answer is"
- Specific numbers: THC %, prices, exact locations, event times

### Retire
- "Solid weed" · "Get buzzed" · "Stay chill" · "Canna-curious" · ALL CAPS for emphasis
- "Premium" used generically (show it, don't say it)
- "Elevate" · "Seamless" · "Unleash" · "Next-level" · "Game-changing"
- "DEAL OF THE DAY" as a visual headline
- "Whether you're a connoisseur or just [X]" — overused, condescending structure

---

## Tagline

**Recommended primary:** "The Eastside Standard."
- Claims the market specifically (Eastside, not generic Seattle)
- Sets a quality bar without stating it explicitly
- Works at any scale: homepage hero, signage, social bio, business card
- Period deliberate — statement of fact, not pitch

**Secondary / campaign use:**
- "Curated for the Eastside." (product-forward moments)
- "Where the Eastside gets together." (community/events)
- "Kirkland's home for cannabis." (local SEO, Google Business, directory listings)

**Retire:** "Stay One Puff Ahead" — pun-forward, budget energy, positions us as deal-seekers not destination-builders.
