# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary — Eastside Regular (28–45).** Kirkland, Redmond, Bothell, Woodinville residents. Educated, employed, comfortable with premium pricing. Experienced cannabis consumers who want quality and convenience without the drive to Seattle. Context: on a laptop or phone, ready to browse the menu before heading in-store. Primary job: find the right product quickly, confirm it's in stock, feel good about the brand they're shopping.

**Secondary — Culture-Adjacent Explorer (21–35).** Follows brands, not just products. Wants to feel like they discovered something worth talking about. Context: social discovery — might find B&B through Instagram or a friend referral. Job: validate that this place is worth their time and spending power.

**Tertiary — Knowledgeable Connoisseur (any age).** Deep product literacy. Wants strain details, terpenes, batch notes, producer intel. Won't tolerate a shallow menu. Job: confirm the curation is real, not marketing.

## Product Purpose

Retail website for Bored and Buzzed Dispensary (Kirkland, WA). Purpose: convert Eastside cannabis search traffic into in-store foot traffic and online order pickups. Success = the site functions as a premium brand statement AND a functional shop. It should make a first-time visitor say "this is the best dispensary I've seen" before they've walked in the door.

## Brand Personality

**Confident. Editorial. Rooted.** "The Culture's Outpost in the Suburbs." Premium and community-forward — the bridge between suburban Eastside living and genuine cannabis culture. Not trying. Not loud. Just the standard.

Tagline: "The Eastside Standard."

Emotions to evoke: **pride** (this is our store), **discovery** (something real here), **ease** (it's just a better experience).

## Anti-references

- Uncle Ike's — functional/commodity, volume-first, 30–50% off banners as primary UX
- Generic "dispensary" template sites — green everywhere, leaf icons, "420 friendly" copy
- Weedmaps iframe menus — low-trust, low-craft, no brand presence
- GNC/supplement retail UX — clinical, transactional, soulless
- Any site that looks like the category-first AI training reflex: "cannabis → warm green + stoner gold + cartoon buds"

## Design Principles

1. **Curation as identity.** The menu is not a catalog — it's a curated statement. Every product shown reinforces that B&B chose it. The UI should reflect choice, not volume.
2. **Premium is earned, not stated.** No "premium" copy. Earn trust through typography precision, spacing, image quality, and product knowledge depth.
3. **The Eastside, specifically.** Geographic specificity is a competitive moat. Kirkland, Redmond, Bothell, Woodinville are named — not "Seattle area." Local = real.
4. **Function first, elevation second.** The menu must be fast, filterable, and trustworthy. Then it must be beautiful. Never sacrifice usability for aesthetics.
5. **Character over template.** Every section should have one design decision that could not have come from a template or AI default. The asymmetry, the editorial crop, the unexpected spacing — this is what makes it a brand, not a website.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum on all text. Body text 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum.
- Muted text (`#8A8070` / `--muted`) on dark background must be checked — secondary labels are the most common failure point.
- All interactive elements: visible focus rings (not `outline: none`).
- Touch targets: 44×44px minimum (dispensary visitors on mobile in a parking lot).
- Age gate must be clearly operable via keyboard (cannabis law requires explicit consent).
- Reduced motion: use `prefers-reduced-motion` for any CSS transitions.
