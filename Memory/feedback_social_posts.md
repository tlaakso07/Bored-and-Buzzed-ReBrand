Always get explicit visual direction before building Instagram posts. See [[MEMORY]] · [[CLAUDE]]

 Session 15 had 4 build/delete cycles with zero specific critique — Trevor deleted everything without explaining what was wrong.

**Why:** Without a reference image or specific brief, every iteration is a guess. Trevor has a clear visual in his head but doesn't always articulate it upfront. The cost of 1 minute of alignment is far lower than 4 rounds of deletes.

**How to apply:** At the start of any social content task:
1. Ask: "Can you share a reference post — screenshot or link — that's close to what you're going for?"
2. OR ask: "Describe one thing you'd definitely want in this post — feel, layout, mood?"
3. Lock in the direction before opening any design tool.

If no reference is available, build ONE rough draft first and explicitly ask "does this direction feel right before I build all 3?" rather than delivering 3 complete versions that might all get deleted.
